Hydroelectric Energy

Hydroelectric energy uses the energy from moving water to generate electricity. It is the oldest and largest renewable energy; commonly seen in dams. Can be used residentially but a flowing water source is needed for sufficient output.

Key Benefits of Hydroelectric Energy

  • Reliable: This resource is completely renewable and reliable due to constant flow of water and only stops working when the water stops flowing.
  • Low Operating Costs: Operational costs are minimal after setup.
  • Environmentally Friendly: Produces no air pollution or greenhouse gas emissions during operation.

Disadvantages

  • Impact on Wildlife: With hydro plants, the running water is damned and the animals surrounding the water are at risk due to the change of ecosystem.
  • Carbon and Methane Emissions: Though there is no emissions that come from the power plant, there is emissions coming from the decaying plants in the water.
  • Flood Risk: Damns are build higher, so there is a flood risk to the surrounding area.

Challenges

Challenges include high initial costs for infrastructure, potential environmental disruptions, and reliance on water availability.
