Solar Energy

Solar energy harnesses the power of the sun to generate electricity and heat. Earth recieves 85 trillion kilowatts of energy from the sun. Which allows the United States gets 500x more than we need.

It is one of the most popular renewable energy sources, offering a clean, sustainable, and cost-effective alternative to fossil fuels. By installing solar panels, users can contribute to reducing carbon emissions while saving on energy costs.

Benefits of Solar Energy

  • Environmental Impact: Solar energy reduces greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution, promoting a cleaner environment.
  • Cost Savings: Solar panels lower electricity bills and provide long-term savings after installation.
  • Energy Independence: Users can generate their own electricity, reducing reliance on traditional energy sources.
  • Job Creation: The solar industry creates numerous jobs in manufacturing, installation, and maintenance.

Disadvantages

  • Harzardous Chemicals:The necessary materials needed to make the panels is highly dangerous.
